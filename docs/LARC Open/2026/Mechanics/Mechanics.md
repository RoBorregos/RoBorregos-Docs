# Mechanical Documentation - LARC Robot 2026
## Hans Velarde

## Mechanical Overview

The different systems in the robot were developed by iterating on the previous version used in 2025 by the team. As with any design process, we first defined the requirements and game strategy that the robot needed to follow.

The full mechanical design and all assemblies were modeled using SolidWorks CAD software.

## Chassis

One of the biggest changes compared to the previous version was replacing the mecanum wheel chassis with an omnidirectional drive system.

The motors are mounted on a rigid MDF base, which provides stability and prevents misalignment, ensuring smooth and precise movement.

### Weight Distribution and Stability

Due to the elevator and storage system height, weight distribution became an important challenge for robot stability and motion consistency.

Encoder feedback became especially important to compensate for small mechanical imbalances and uneven weight distribution, helping maintain smoother and more stable movement during autonomous navigation.

## Elevator

The elevator system uses a piston driven by a 12V DC motor.

## Collection System

Based on the strategy of collecting two levels in a single pass, the collection system was designed to capture coffee beans from different heights.

## Classification System

This subsystem connects the collection system with storage and is responsible for sorting coffee beans into category A or B.

## Storage System

The storage system consists of two MDF containers made with laser cutting.

## Manufacturing Process

Most structural MDF components were laser cut to ensure repeatability and fast manufacturing.

Custom sensor holders and guiding mechanisms were 3D printed using PLA, allowing rapid prototyping and iterative testing during development.

## Maintenance and Accessibility

The modular structure simplified maintenance and debugging during competitions, allowing damaged or malfunctioning subsystems to be replaced independently without disassembling the entire robot.

## Mechanical Improvements and Suggestions

- Consider buying new motors.
- Replace worn omni wheels.
- Improve cable management.
- Redesign the sorting system.
- Improve accessibility of sensor mounts.
