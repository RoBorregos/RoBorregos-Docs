\section{Mechanical and Structural Design}

\subsection{General Construction}

The robot structure was designed prioritizing mechanical resistance and proper mass
distribution. The black components were manufactured using \textbf{ABS}, while the white
components were manufactured using \textbf{PLA}.

The primary objective was for most of the robot body to be built using ABS due to its
higher rigidity and impact resistance during competition. This allowed the structure to
better withstand collisions and mechanical stress.

\vspace{8pt}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
\textbf{Component} & \textbf{Material} & \textbf{Purpose} \\
\midrule
Black parts & ABS & Higher structural resistance \\[4pt]
White parts & PLA & Secondary components \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\subsection{Mass Distribution}

One of the main design challenges was integrating the 12\,V LiPo battery. Due to its
weight, this component had a major influence on the robot's center of mass.

It was observed that placing the battery away from the center produced significant
variations in the robot's dynamic balance. For this reason, a dedicated support structure
was designed to position the battery at the center of the robot.

Battery access is achieved through a removable section of the structure, which can be
disassembled and reassembled using screws and nuts.

\begin{notebox}
\textbf{Note:} No comparative tests were performed using different battery positions in
order to quantify the effect on the center of mass.
\end{notebox}

\section{Structural System}

\subsection{Motor Supports}

The connection between the first and second levels of the robot is achieved through the
motor supports. These supports simultaneously fulfill structural and mounting functions.

The assembly process is performed as follows:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item The supports are screwed from the top side to attach them to the second level.
    \item They are then fixed from the bottom side to connect them to the first level.
    \item This configuration increases the rigidity of the overall structure.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Ring Support System}

The ring is supported using spacers directly connected to the main support structure.
Additionally, the system is reinforced using screws located at the top section.

This design helps maintain the stability of the ring during operation and absorbs stresses
generated by impacts.

\section{Physical Dimensions}

The robot has an approximate height of 21.7\,cm and a total mass of approximately
1330\,g. These dimensions were selected to maintain a balance between structural rigidity,
stability, and maneuverability during operation and competition.

\section{Outer Shell}

\subsection{Shell Design}

The outer shell was designed as a single continuous piece. This decision allowed impact
energy to be distributed more effectively while reducing the probability of structural
fractures.

The main advantages of this design include:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Increased structural rigidity.
    \item Improved mechanical stress distribution.
    \item Reduced impact damage.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Design Limitations}

Despite its advantages, the design also introduced several limitations.

Since the shell consists of a single piece, accessing lower-level internal components
such as the battery and phototransistors becomes considerably more difficult,
complicating maintenance and assembly tasks.

Additionally, due to the low ground clearance, the robot experienced difficulties when
moving across uneven surfaces or slopes, occasionally getting stuck during testing and
matches.

\subsection{Visual Interference Reduction}

One of the secondary objectives of the shell was to partially cover the light emitted by
the LEDs and phototransistors in order to reduce potential visual interference.

\begin{notebox}
\textbf{Note:} This aspect was not considered critical within the Soccer IR category,
since multiple teams used red LEDs without presenting significant issues during the
competition.
\end{notebox}
